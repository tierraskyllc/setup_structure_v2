L.A.M.A. History

The Latin American Motorcycle Association (L.A.M.A) was founded in 1977 in Chicago's notorious Humboldt Park neighborhood. From its inception, L.A.M.A. was intended to be a Moto-Touring club with truly Democratic ideas. A lone chapter until the first chapter was started in Miami in 1995, L.A.M.A became a National Association when the first national president was elected in 1996.

In 1999, L.A.M.A. became an international association with chapters in Puerto Rico, Mexico, and the U.S.A. & Cuba. Presently, L.A.M.A. has additional chapters in Uruguay, Venezuela, Argentina, Spain & Australia. LAMA was intended to be the club of the 21st century and is a work in progress!
